# Branding — Voice, Tone & Personality

> **Purpose:** How Orgofin sounds in writing — the counterpart to `design-system.md` (how it looks). Governs every headline, button label, error message, and email.
> **Applies to:** anyone (human or Claude) writing any user-facing copy.

## Responsibilities

Owns voice, tone, and brand personality. Does not own the actual page-by-page copy (that's [`docs/product/copy.md`](../../docs/product/copy.md) — a specific instance of this voice applied to specific pages) or visual identity (`design-system.md`).

## Voice & Tone (working rules)

- **Confident, not hype-y.** State facts plainly ("A 200-person company spends ₹1.5Cr/year on fragmented software") rather than reaching for adjectives ("incredible," "revolutionary," "game-changing").
- **Short sentences. Then room to breathe.** Cinematic pacing — one idea per line, not paragraphs of persuasion stacked together.
- **India-proud, globally ambitious.** Compliance depth (GST, PF, ESI, DPDP) is stated as an advantage, never apologized for, hedged, or buried beneath the global ambition.
- **Plain English before jargon.** "Company Brain" and "AGaaS" always get a one-sentence plain-language translation before or alongside the term — see the Company Brain hero copy in `docs/product/copy.md` §2 for the reference example.
- **Show, don't sell.** Prefer the CEO Intelligence Agent vignette style — concrete, specific, almost boring in its factual detail — over adjectives about "the future of work."

## Brand Personality _(synthesized, unconfirmed — see status flag above)_

Inferred from the tone and word choice of the source vision document:

- **Category-defining, not incremental** — "we don't build tools, we build the brain."
- **Anti-fragmentation, pro-consolidation** — near-evangelical about "one subscription" replacing vendor sprawl.
- **Technically confident / architecture-forward** — leads with graph/event/agent architecture even in investor-facing material, unusual restraint from generic hype language.
- **Outcome-obsessed, not feature-obsessed** — the CEO Intelligence Agent narrative sells a _result_ (a morning briefing that already happened), not a UI.

## What this voice should never do

- Never use filler SaaS adjectives ("seamless," "revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "game-changing") as a substitute for a concrete claim.
- Never introduce a second typeface-equivalent in writing — i.e., don't mix this confident/plain voice with a second register (e.g., a jokey/casual register for microcopy and a formal register for headlines). One voice, adjusted in formality only slightly between a headline and a form error message.
- Never let "Company Brain" or "AGaaS" go three consecutive sentences without being anchored back to something concrete a reader already understands.
