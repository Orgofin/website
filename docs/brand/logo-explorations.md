# Orgofin — Logo Explorations (15 Directions)

> **Purpose:** A broad second round of original logo directions for Orgofin — futuristic, iconic, and built to scale from a 16px favicon to a billboard. Companion to the first four concepts in [`logo-concepts.md`](./logo-concepts.md); this round pushes further toward the simplicity and timelessness of Stripe / Linear / Notion / OpenAI / Anthropic.
> **How to view:** open [`explorations/preview.html`](./explorations/preview.html) — every concept is shown as a logo lockup, monochrome, app icon, and 32/16px favicon, on both light and dark.
> **Classification:** Internal (pre-decision). Proposals only — not an adopted brand.

---

## Responsibilities

Owns the expanded logo exploration and per-concept rationale. Does not select the final mark (founder decision) or own the design tokens/voice. Each mark is a single `currentColor` SVG in [`explorations/`](./explorations/), so it recolors to any brand color and exports to mono/reversed without redrawing — the way a real icon system ships.

---

## Design principles applied

- **One idea, one shape.** Each mark expresses a single concept a viewer can "get." No composite busyness.
- **Geometric construction.** Built on a 48-unit grid from circles, lines, and simple paths — reproducible, scalable, timeless.
- **Favicon-first.** Every mark is checked at 16px; anything that muddies is simplified.
- **Mono-native.** Designed in one color first (`currentColor`); color/gradient is an enhancement, not a crutch — exactly how Stripe/Linear/Notion/Anthropic marks behave.
- **No finance clichés.** Zero dollar signs, coins, banks, bar/candlestick charts, or generic AI sparkles. Trust, intelligence, organization, flow, and growth are expressed _abstractly_.

Colors in the preview (indigo→violet `#4F46E5 → #7C3AED`) and the Geist wordmark are **placeholders** — decide these alongside the mark and the in-progress brand-palette experiment.

## The 15 concepts

Grouped by the idea they build on. ★ ratings are my honest read on the requested axes.

### O-based (the letter as a system)

**01 · Aperture** — `explorations/c01-aperture-o.svg`

- **Represents:** the O as a camera aperture — a ring segmented into three blades, slightly rotated for motion.
- **Why Orgofin:** "open, precise, adaptive organization." Openness + control without any finance signifier.
- **Strengths:** exceptional at 16px; feels engineered and modern; single-stroke = trivially scalable/animatable. **Drawbacks:** aperture rings exist elsewhere (cameras, some tech brands) — distinctiveness rests on the exact segmentation.
- **Best for:** favicon, app icon, animated loader (blades rotating). _Originality ★★★ · Simplicity ★★★★★ · Scalability ★★★★★_

**02 · Nodegraph** — `explorations/c02-nodegraph-o.svg`

- **Represents:** the O as four connected nodes with a bright center — a graph.
- **Why Orgofin:** the _most on-message_ mark. Orgofin builds the Company Brain graph first; this is that idea.
- **Strengths:** directly communicates the product; ownable; extends into a full graph visual system. **Drawbacks:** node-graph marks are common in data/AI; five dots is near the 16px detail ceiling (the mark trims for small sizes).
- **Best for:** website, decks, section motifs; strong app icon. _Originality ★★★ · Simplicity ★★★★ · Scalability ★★★★_

**03 · Orbit** — `explorations/c03-orbit-o.svg`

- **Represents:** a central core with one agent in orbit.
- **Why Orgofin:** the AGaaS story — one brain, autonomous agents around it — in the simplest possible glyph.
- **Strengths:** minimal, kinetic, unmistakably an "O"; great favicon. **Drawbacks:** atom/orbit marks are well-trodden; needs a distinctive execution to avoid "generic tech."
- **Best for:** favicon, app icon, motion. _Originality ★★★ · Simplicity ★★★★★ · Scalability ★★★★★_

**04 · Interlock** — `explorations/c04-interlock-o.svg`

- **Represents:** two overlapping rings becoming one.
- **Why Orgofin:** unification — 20–80 fragmented tools resolving into one system; also reads as collaboration and ∞.
- **Strengths:** warm, human, memorable; simple two-circle geometry. **Drawbacks:** "linked rings" is used by several brands (Mastercard-adjacent, audio apps) — risk of familiarity; less overtly "AI."
- **Best for:** brand/marketing, partnerships storytelling. _Originality ★★ · Simplicity ★★★★★ · Scalability ★★★★★_

**05 · Constellation** — `explorations/c05-grid-dissolve-o.svg`

- **Represents:** an O of dots resolving from small/faint to large/solid — order from data.
- **Why Orgofin:** clarity emerging from scattered, fragmented information — the core promise.
- **Strengths:** distinctive and premium; beautiful in motion (dots "resolving"). **Drawbacks:** the most detailed here — the gradient-of-dots simplifies to a plain dotted ring at 16px.
- **Best for:** hero/website, decks, loading animation; favicon works but loses nuance. _Originality ★★★★ · Simplicity ★★★ · Scalability ★★★_

### O + F (monogram territory)

**06 · Monogram OF** — `explorations/c06-of-ligature.svg`

- **Represents:** an F nested inside the O — the two initials as one lockup.
- **Why Orgofin:** literal brand initials; enterprise-serious; unambiguous.
- **Strengths:** classic, trustworthy, unmistakably "Orgofin Finance." **Drawbacks:** letter-in-a-ring is a common monogram pattern; the least "next-gen" of the set.
- **Best for:** decks, documents, formal/enterprise contexts, favicon. _Originality ★★ · Simplicity ★★★★ · Scalability ★★★★_

**07 · Eclipse** — `explorations/c07-negspace-f.svg`

- **Represents:** a solid disc with an **F carved out in negative space**.
- **Why Orgofin:** the negative-space route the brief asked for — confident, iconic, "fills the tile."
- **Strengths:** the most _ownable and app-icon-native_ concept (cf. filled-tile marks like Notion/Linear); bold on any background; unmistakable at 16px. **Drawbacks:** the F must be optically tuned so the counter reads cleanly small; a filled disc is a strong commitment.
- **Best for:** app icon and favicon above all; excellent everywhere. _Originality ★★★★ · Simplicity ★★★★★ · Scalability ★★★★★_

### F-based (finance, flow, structure)

**08 · Flow** — `explorations/c08-flow-f.svg`

- **Represents:** a warm, rounded F — finance and workflows in motion.
- **Why Orgofin:** "financial workflows" as _software_, not a bank; approachable and human.
- **Strengths:** friendly, legible, timeless; trivial to scale. **Drawbacks:** a plain F is less conceptually rich; leans on typography/color for distinctiveness.
- **Best for:** favicon, app icon, product UI. _Originality ★★ · Simplicity ★★★★★ · Scalability ★★★★★_

**09 · Vector** — `explorations/c09-chevron-forward.svg`

- **Represents:** nested forward chevrons — momentum and flow.
- **Why Orgofin:** capital and work moving forward; speed and progress.
- **Strengths:** energetic, simple, scales perfectly. **Drawbacks:** chevrons are extremely common (media players, many brands) — low distinctiveness on its own.
- **Best for:** supporting/UI iconography more than a primary logo. _Originality ★★ · Simplicity ★★★★★ · Scalability ★★★★★_

**10 · Ledger** — `explorations/c10-folder-o.svg`

- **Represents:** a folder with a node at its center — structured documents + intelligence.
- **Why Orgofin:** organization and financial documents, subtly, with the "brain" node inside.
- **Strengths:** communicates the domain without a cliché; clear metaphor. **Drawbacks:** folder marks can feel utilitarian/skeuomorphic; less "futuristic" than the abstract set.
- **Best for:** product UI, docs features; usable favicon. _Originality ★★★ · Simplicity ★★★★ · Scalability ★★★★_

### Abstract / futuristic

**11 · Cube** — `explorations/c11-iso-cube.svg`

- **Represents:** an isometric cube — dimensional, structured, foundational.
- **Why Orgofin:** the "Enterprise **OS**" — a system with depth and structure.
- **Strengths:** futuristic, abstract, becomes recognizable over time (the OpenAI/Airbnb pattern); great app icon. **Drawbacks:** iso cubes are used by dev/infra brands; the three inner edges thin out at 16px.
- **Best for:** app icon, decks, developer-facing surfaces. _Originality ★★★ · Simplicity ★★★★ · Scalability ★★★★_

**12 · Prism** — `explorations/c12-prism.svg`

- **Represents:** a prism refracting one input into a clear spectrum.
- **Why Orgofin:** raw data → insight; diligence and intelligence, expressed abstractly.
- **Strengths:** premium, distinctive, "smart"; a memorable idea once learned. **Drawbacks:** meaning needs a beat; the diverging rays are the most fragile element at 16px.
- **Best for:** brand/website, decks, thought-leadership. _Originality ★★★★★ · Simplicity ★★★ · Scalability ★★★_

**13 · Pulse** — `explorations/c13-pulse.svg`

- **Represents:** a core broadcasting concentric signals.
- **Why Orgofin:** a living, thinking brain acting in real time — intelligence and agents.
- **Strengths:** implies motion/intelligence; clean; nice as an animated "thinking" state. **Drawbacks:** signal/wifi-adjacent shapes are common; asymmetric, so centering in a tile needs care.
- **Best for:** motion, status/loading, app icon. _Originality ★★★ · Simplicity ★★★★ · Scalability ★★★★_

**14 · Ascend** — `explorations/c14-ascending-nodes.svg`

- **Represents:** rising, connected nodes — growth as a network path.
- **Why Orgofin:** growth and data network effects _without_ a bar/candlestick chart.
- **Strengths:** optimistic, clearly software, communicates growth honestly. **Drawbacks:** rising-line marks are common in analytics/fintech — must be executed to feel distinct; directional.
- **Best for:** website, investor decks; usable favicon. _Originality ★★★ · Simplicity ★★★★ · Scalability ★★★★_

**15 · Keystone** — `explorations/c15-keystone.svg`

- **Represents:** an arch with an aperture keystone — the load-bearing center that holds everything.
- **Why Orgofin:** "the keystone of your company" — trust, foundation, and openness, abstractly.
- **Strengths:** unique metaphor; conveys trust/diligence without a bank/vault cliché. **Drawbacks:** arch can read as architecture/insurance; the aperture detail softens at 16px.
- **Best for:** enterprise/brand contexts, decks. _Originality ★★★★ · Simplicity ★★★★ · Scalability ★★★★_

## Shortlist & recommendation

If the goal is a mark that is **original, dead-simple, and scales forever**, my top four to take into refinement:

1. **07 Eclipse** — the strongest _iconic/app-icon_ route; fills the tile, owns negative space, unmistakable at 16px.
2. **02 Nodegraph** — the most _on-strategy_ (the Company Brain graph is the company); best foundation for a whole visual system.
3. **01 Aperture** — the cleanest, most _timeless_ pure-geometry O; superb small and in motion.
4. **12 Prism** — the most _distinctive idea_ if the founders want a mark that rewards a second look (OpenAI/Stripe-style abstraction).

A powerful combination: pick **Eclipse or Aperture as the primary mark**, and use **Nodegraph's graph language as the supporting visual system** (backgrounds, section dividers, the existing `CompanyBrainGraph`, loaders). That gives one crisp, ownable symbol plus a rich motif that tells the Company-Brain story everywhere else.

This is a recommendation — all 15 are provided so the founders can compare and choose.

## Next steps (post-decision)

1. Founders review [`explorations/preview.html`](./explorations/preview.html) (+ the first round) and shortlist 2–3.
2. Refine the chosen mark with a designer: optical corrections, exact grid, wordmark kerning, and a locked color/gradient (decide with the palette experiment).
3. Produce the asset set: `favicon.ico` (multi-size), `favicon.svg`, `apple-touch-icon.png` (180), PWA `icon-192/512.png`, `logo.svg`/`logo.png`, and **`/og/default.png`** — the last two also close launch blocker **B-01** ([production-readiness review](../launch/production-readiness-review.md)).
4. Replace `src/app/favicon.ico`, add `public/logo.png` + `public/og/default.png`, update `siteConfig`.

> **Business-fact discipline:** these are proposals. Do not present any as "the Orgofin logo" externally until the founders select and finalize one.
